Who is

Ro de Rivero

He is an experienced Peruvian interior designer. With a detailed and nomadic impulse, he began his career at the age of 19 in Naples (Florida), within the renowned furniture company Roche Bobois. His work would later take him to cities such as Los Angeles, New York, Bogota and Mexico City, environments that shaped his timeless style with a strong dramatic charge.

Graduated in Interior Design at Toulouse Lautrec and with advanced studies at the Fashion Institute of Technology, he has worked for Demi Moore, Dylan Mc Dermont, Sofia Vergara and Unicef. Her designs privilege the client's personality, projecting in the space a reflection of their inner layers.

He participated in the first edition of Casacor Peru, an exhibition of which he is a regular member. He founded Studio Ro de Rivero, one of the most renowned interior design studios in Lima. He is also the owner of Monocromo, a furniture and ornaments store focused on creating unique pieces for universal experiences.

What has said

The Press

Ro de Rivero's work has not been out of the eye of the specialized press. Casas magazine reviewed one of her designs as a "proposal focused on transmitting with decorative pieces that become transcendental in every space".

Several profiles and articles have been written about his work and his creative process. Texts where her "cosmopolitan and timeless" style is emphasized, as well as her focus on creating "client-centered spaces".

What we do

The Services

Studio Ro de Rivero offers services in the areas of hospitality, commercial and residential. Framed in a cosmopolitan and particular character, each area encompasses a world of different dynamics.

Hotel Projects

Absorbing the personality of their environment to create particular experiences, the hotels designed by Ro de Rivero are an amalgam of tradition and rupture, such as Holiday inn Lima and Holiday inn Piura, and an oasis within the city, such as Boutique Attalea.

Commercial Projects

The Vichayito complex was designed as a space of connection with nature, nourished by the pulse of the sea, natural light and minimalist lines. While in Roosevelt -another of its commercial projects- the contrast between the elegance of the living towers and the vigorous movement of the offices prevails.

Residential Projects

The details tell a story that the light dramatizes. The needs of the environment rule in Ro de Rivero's residential projects. One such case is La Huaca, where soft contrast accentuates a cozy day of retreat; or Lithos, where harmony is produced through a torrent of colors, shapes and textures.
